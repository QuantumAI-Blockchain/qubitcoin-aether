## Agent-Specific Threat Awareness

### THREAT-LIST-01: Fake Exchange Scams
Fraudulent exchanges may approach QBC to extract listing fees without providing real service.
- Mitigation: Verify exchange legitimacy: regulatory status, verifiable volume, community reputation.
- Mitigation: Never pay upfront listing fees without due diligence.
- Mitigation: Consult knowledge-worker for exchange verification.

### THREAT-LIST-02: Liquidity Drain Attacks
Malicious actors may drain liquidity pools through flash loan attacks or manipulation.
- Mitigation: Monitor pool health in real-time. Alert security on unusual activity.
- Mitigation: Deploy pools with appropriate protections: TWAP oracles, withdrawal limits.
- Mitigation: Coordinate with security agent for pool monitoring.

### THREAT-LIST-03: Exchange Delisting Risk
Exchanges may delist tokens that fail to meet ongoing requirements (volume, compliance, fees).
- Mitigation: Track all ongoing requirements per exchange.
- Mitigation: Coordinate with trader to maintain minimum volume thresholds.
- Mitigation: Maintain compliance documentation proactively.

### THREAT-LIST-04: Bridge Exploitation
Cross-chain bridges are frequent attack targets. A bridge exploit could drain QBC from wrapped pools.
- Mitigation: Bridge contracts audited by bug-hunter before deployment.
- Mitigation: Bridge liquidity limits: do not over-commit to any single bridge.
- Mitigation: Security agent monitors bridge transactions for anomalies.

### THREAT-LIST-05: Market Manipulation Accusations
Concentrated trading or irregular patterns could attract regulatory attention.
- Mitigation: All market making and liquidity provision is transparent and documented.
- Mitigation: No wash trading, no spoofing, no layering. Clean market behavior only.
- Mitigation: Maintain records that demonstrate legitimate market making.
