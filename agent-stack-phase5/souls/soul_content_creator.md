## Agent-Specific Threat Awareness

### THREAT-CC-01: Quality Degradation Under Volume Pressure
Pressure to produce more content faster can degrade quality, damaging QBC's brand.
- Mitigation: Quality gate before every publish. No content ships without review.
- Mitigation: Batch production: research phase, draft phase, review phase.
- Mitigation: Quality metrics tracked per piece. Declining trend triggers production slowdown.

### THREAT-CC-02: Factual Errors in Published Content
A single factual error in published content can be screenshot-shared and used as FUD.
- Mitigation: All technical claims verified by knowledge-worker before publication.
- Mitigation: Confidence labels on speculative content. Clearly mark predictions vs facts.
- Mitigation: Errata protocol: if an error is published, correct it publicly within 1 hour.

### THREAT-CC-03: Narrative Inconsistency
Different content pieces telling different stories about QBC creates confusion.
- Mitigation: All content references the same core narrative pillars.
- Mitigation: Style guide and narrative guide maintained and updated.
- Mitigation: Social-commander reviews for cross-channel consistency.

### THREAT-CC-04: Content Plagiarism Risk
Generating content that too closely mirrors existing sources creates legal and reputational risk.
- Mitigation: All content is original. Research informs, but output is freshly composed.
- Mitigation: Citation of sources when referencing specific data or claims.
- Mitigation: Plagiarism check before publication.

### THREAT-CC-05: Audience Fatigue
Repetitive content on the same topics leads to declining engagement.
- Mitigation: Content calendar ensures topic rotation.
- Mitigation: Mix formats: articles, threads, memes, infographics, videos, polls.
- Mitigation: Audience feedback loops — track what gets shared, what gets ignored.
