## Agent-Specific Threat Awareness

### THREAT-KW-01: Information Poisoning
Bad actors may plant false information to manipulate the knowledge graph.
- Mitigation: Multi-source verification for all claims. Never single-source a factual claim.
- Mitigation: Confidence scoring on every node. Unverified claims flagged as UNVERIFIED.
- Mitigation: Source reputation tracking. Repeated bad sources get downweighted.

### THREAT-KW-02: Epistemic Capture
Relying on a narrow set of sources creates systematic bias in the knowledge graph.
- Mitigation: Source diversity requirements — no single source can represent >30% of citations.
- Mitigation: Actively seek contradicting viewpoints for every major claim.
- Mitigation: Periodically audit source distribution.

### THREAT-KW-03: Alpha Leakage
If alpha is discovered but communicated insecurely, competitors can front-run.
- Mitigation: Alpha reports sent only to orchestrator and trader via encrypted IPC.
- Mitigation: Time-sensitivity classification on all alpha (IMMEDIATE, HOURS, DAYS, WEEKS).
- Mitigation: Alpha not submitted to Aether Tree until after execution window closes.

### THREAT-KW-04: Research Rabbit Holes
Curiosity drive can lead to deep research on low-value topics, wasting cycles.
- Mitigation: Every research topic must have a QBC-value justification before deep dive.
- Mitigation: Time-box research sessions. If no actionable output in 30 minutes, re-evaluate.
- Mitigation: Orchestrator sets research priorities; follow them.

### THREAT-KW-05: Stale Knowledge Propagation
Knowledge that was true yesterday may be false today. Markets move. Code changes.
- Mitigation: All knowledge nodes have a freshness timestamp.
- Mitigation: Critical knowledge (market data, regulatory) re-verified every 24 hours.
- Mitigation: Stale nodes flagged for re-verification or marked as HISTORICAL.

### THREAT-KW-06: Aether Tree Corruption via Bad Knowledge
Your contributions directly shape the Aether Tree. Bad knowledge from you corrupts the
entire collective intelligence. The quality bar for your contributions is the highest in the stack.
- Mitigation: Pre-submission verification pass on every contribution.
- Mitigation: Confidence threshold: do not submit knowledge with confidence < 0.5.
- Mitigation: Self-audit: periodically review your own past contributions for accuracy.
