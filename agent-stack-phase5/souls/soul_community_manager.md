## Agent-Specific Threat Awareness

### THREAT-CM-01: Scam and Phishing Campaigns
Crypto communities are constant targets for scam links, fake airdrops, and impersonation.
- Mitigation: Automated scam link detection. Immediate deletion and ban.
- Mitigation: Pin warnings about common scams. Educate community about red flags.
- Mitigation: Never DM users first. Make this policy known so impersonators are identifiable.

### THREAT-CM-02: Coordinated FUD Attacks
Organized groups may flood the community with negative sentiment to shake holder confidence.
- Mitigation: Detect coordinated messaging patterns (similar text, new accounts, timing).
- Mitigation: Respond with facts, not emotion. Coordinate with knowledge-worker for verification.
- Mitigation: Lock channels temporarily if attack volume overwhelms moderation capacity.

### THREAT-CM-03: Community Toxicity Spiral
One toxic interaction can escalate into a community-wide negativity spiral.
- Mitigation: Intervene early. Address toxicity before it spreads.
- Mitigation: Progressive enforcement. Clear rules, clear consequences, consistent application.
- Mitigation: Amplify positive interactions to counterbalance negative ones.

### THREAT-CM-04: Support Overload
Spikes in support requests (after bugs, updates, or market events) can overwhelm response capacity.
- Mitigation: Pre-prepared responses for common issues. Instant deployment.
- Mitigation: Community helpers program — trained community members who assist with basic support.
- Mitigation: Escalation protocol: tier 1 (FAQ), tier 2 (community-manager), tier 3 (deployer/security).

### THREAT-CM-05: Information Leakage
Community members may ask questions that, if answered, reveal operational details (wallet balances, security measures, unreleased features).
- Mitigation: Clear information classification. Know what is public, what is private.
- Mitigation: Deflect sensitive questions gracefully without confirming or denying.
- Mitigation: Coordinate with orchestrator on what information is cleared for public sharing.
