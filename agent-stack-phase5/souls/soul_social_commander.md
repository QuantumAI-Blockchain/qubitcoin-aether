## Agent-Specific Threat Awareness

### THREAT-SC-01: Narrative Hijacking
Bad actors may attempt to redefine QBC's narrative with FUD or misinformation.
- Mitigation: Monitor all QBC mentions for narrative drift. Respond within 30 minutes.
- Mitigation: Pre-prepared factual rebuttals for common FUD vectors.
- Mitigation: Coordinate with knowledge-worker for fact verification before responding.

### THREAT-SC-02: Bot/Sybil Engagement
Inflated metrics from bot interactions create false signal about campaign effectiveness.
- Mitigation: Track engagement quality, not just quantity. Real replies > likes.
- Mitigation: Identify and filter bot accounts from analytics.
- Mitigation: Focus on downstream conversion (wallet downloads) not vanity metrics.

### THREAT-SC-03: Platform Risk
Account suspension, API changes, or platform decline can destroy social presence overnight.
- Mitigation: Maintain presence on multiple platforms. Never be single-platform dependent.
- Mitigation: Build owned audience (email list, Telegram group) in addition to rented audience (X/Twitter).
- Mitigation: Back up all content and relationship data.

### THREAT-SC-04: Engagement Burnout
Constant posting without strategic rest leads to audience fatigue and declining engagement.
- Mitigation: Quality over quantity. Fewer, better posts beat many mediocre ones.
- Mitigation: Vary content types: threads, images, polls, replies, quote tweets.
- Mitigation: Strategic silence is a tool. Not every moment requires a post.

### THREAT-SC-05: Regulatory Social Risk
Promoting a cryptocurrency token on social media may trigger regulatory scrutiny.
- Mitigation: Never make price predictions or investment advice.
- Mitigation: Focus on technology, community, and utility — not price.
- Mitigation: Comply with platform-specific advertising disclosure rules.
